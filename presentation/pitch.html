Hook

Every year, 2 to 5 percent of global GDP — up to two trillion dollars — is laundered. And only about 1 percent is ever caught.

Why? Because the people moving that money never put their own name on the payment. They hide behind shell companies, stand-ins, and chains of accounts. A bank checks the name against a sanctions list, sees nothing, and lets it through.

0:30
What it is

We're ctrl alt elite, and we built ScreenSmart — real-time sanctions screening that doesn't just check the name, it follows the money. Every payment gets a MATCH, REVIEW, or NO-MATCH verdict in under a second, streamed live to an analyst dashboard.

1:00
How it works

Two engines screen every payment in parallel. The first is name & identity — a machine-learning model that catches typos, transliterations and aliases; "Qadhafi" spelled five different ways.

The second is the network engine: it traces the account through a graph of counterparties to find hidden links — payee, to an intermediary, to a sanctioned entity two hops away. The results are combined, worst-case wins, and the payment is flagged with a full evidence chain.

And because an analyst shouldn't need a data-science degree, a local AI model — running entirely on-device — explains, in plain English, exactly why each payment was flagged.
